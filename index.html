Observation 1

The AI boom is driving up critical input costs

  • Electricity consumption by AI data centers has grown dramatically, pushing power prices higher in key regions.
  • Semiconductor materials, rare earths, and related components are seeing sustained price pressure due to strong demand.
  • Major technology companies are competing for the same limited supply chains.

Observation 3

Major banks continue to recommend heavy AI exposure

Many financial institutions are encouraging clients to increase allocations to AI-related stocks and infrastructure, despite rapidly rising valuations.

“History suggests that when banks broadly promote the same concentrated theme, it is worth examining concentration risk carefully.”

Observation 4

U.S. housing affordability remains under significant pressure

Mortgage delinquencies and the share of households struggling with payments have reached levels not seen since the 2007–2008 period.
  • Many homeowners are highly leveraged with limited buffer for economic shocks.
  • Any meaningful rise in unemployment or interest rate pressure could trigger forced selling.
  • Such forced liquidation often spills over into equity markets, including technology stocks.

Early Indicators

Supply chain disruptions already impacting the sector

Recent geopolitical tensions (including conflicts affecting fertilizer and chemical imports) have created shortages of nitrogen-based compounds used in semiconductor fabrication cooling processes.

Even minor disruptions in critical inputs can quickly affect production timelines and investor sentiment.
🔄

Potential Feedback Loop

Rising input costs → Production constraints → Margin pressure on tech companies → Investor disappointment → Equity selling → Liquidity pressure on leveraged households → Further equity selling
This cycle, once started, can accelerate quickly.
STRATEGIC OPPORTUNITY

Positioning ahead of the market

Sophisticated investors in 2007 identified overvalued assets and took defensive or opportunistic positions before the broader market adjusted.

Key takeaway
While the AI theme offers genuine long-term potential, current valuations and systemic leverage warrant careful risk management.

Exploring hedging strategies or selective short positions in highly concentrated areas may allow investors to protect capital and potentially benefit from a correction.
